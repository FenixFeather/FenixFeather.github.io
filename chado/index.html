1 Answers

1.1 Understand the major religions practiced in Japan – name, origin, basic concepts and what they worship.

  • Shinto
    • Origin: Japan
    • Basic concepts:
      • Nature
      • Kami, or spirits, are in everything
      • Focus on the present
  • Buddhism
    • Origin: China
    • Basic concepts
      • Enlightenment by detachment from desires
      • The four noble truths
      • The Eightfold Path
      • Focus on more spiritual things

1.2 The Zen arts: Chado, Kado, sumi-e, Noh, bushido, shodo

  • Chado
    • Chado is the way of the tea.
  • Kado
    • The way of flowers
    • Includes Chabana and Ikebana
  • Sumi-e
    • Brush painting
  • Noh
    • Japanese theatre form
    • Uses masks
  • Bushido
    • The way of the Samurai
  • Shodo
    • Way of the calligraphy

1.3 Origins of tea, who drank it, what it was used for, and who were the primary individuals that developed it further

  • Origins of tea
    • Came from China
    • Drunk by nobles and elites and stuff
    • Zen monks formalized the consumption of tea as an art form
      • Used as a meditation aid
  • Primary individuals: See historical individuals section.
    • Sen Rikyu
    • Jo-o
    • Murata Shuko

1.4 What is Wa, Kei, Sei, and Jaku and how does the Way of Tea embody each of them?

  • Wa: Harmony
    • The formulaic way tea is served is pretty harmonious
  • Kei: Respect
    • Tea is very concerned with the relation between guest and host
  • Sei: Purity
    • You have to purify yourself with the Sukubai
    • Cleaning the instruments and tools is all part of Tea
  • Jaku: Tranquility
    • Tea is a meditative experience.
    • Lets you open your mind through ritual.

1.5 Know what role the tea garden plays for a tea ceremony and the basic aspects of it.

  • I think you’re supposed to walk through it and consider nature and stuff
  • Called Chaniwa
  • Stone path known as roji
  • the tea garden is created in such a way that those who enter feel as if they are far away from the din and dust of the mundane outer world. It should provide an atmosphere of harmony with nature and one that is conducive to a meditative frame of mind.
  • Purification before tea

1.6 What is the name and functionality behind the small tea door entrance and who developed it?

  • Name: nijiri-guchi
  • Functionality: To function as a filter that strips away all material differences and only leaves the human being behind.
  • Developer: Sen Rikyu

1.7 Know the basic characteristics of Chabana and understand its difference from Ikebana

  • Chabana is simple and elegant, whereas Ikebana is more ostentatious.
  • Chabana is free and natural, not created, placed in tokonoma, neutral light brown walls, simple platform, must be fresh flowers, do not crowd opening, flowers face the viewer, number of flowers odd, everything lies naturally (Andrew).

1.10 Understand the difference between Kabuki and Noh

  • Kabuki is very elaborate and stylized whereas Noh is very subdued and has very little motion.
  • Noh can be hard to understand and figure out and lasts for hours. Not everyone’s cup of tea.

1.14 Know basic terms of Japanese architecture

  • Tatami: thick strawfloor floor mats bound with cotton or hemp. Room size is discussed in terms of tatami, sort of like how we discuss size in terms of square feet. 6’ by 3’.
  • Fusumu: Decorated sliding partitions that divide rooms. Wod lattice core framework, covered with heavy paper.
  • Shoji: Door, window or room divider consisting of translucent rice paper over a frame of wood which holds together a lattice of wood or bamboo. (Wikipedia editors, CC BY-SA)
  • Tokonoma: The little enclosed alcove where we look at flowers and scrolls. Most sacred area of the room.

1.15 Know the basic four beauties of Japanese aesthetics and their meanings

  • Hade: Very ostentatious and heavily engineered beauty
  • Iki: Sleek, hip, stylish
  • Shibui: Elegant, simple
  • Wabi Sabi: Aging, quaint

1.16 Know the following historical individuals and their significance

  • Hideyoshi – Finished the unification of Japan. Tried and failed to invade China twice.
  • Hirohito – Emperor who presided over Japan’s rise to power, participation in WWII, and the post-war rebuilding.
  • Shuko – Came up with the concept that tea went beyond entertainment, medicinal value, or temple ceremony; that the preparation and drinking of tea could be an expression of the Zen belief that every act of daily life is a potential act that can lead to enlightenment.
  • Takeno Joo – Joo broadened the practice of Tea.
  • Rikyu – A disciple of Joo, Rikyu was very influential in making tea more Zen and appreciation of things that are wabi-sabi.
  • Nobunga – Beginning of the end of the warring states

1.17 Know the Japanese names of the basic tea equipment

  • Kama: Hot water container
  • Mizusashi: Cold water container
  • Hishaku: Water dipper
  • Natsume: Tea caddy
  • Chasen: Tea whisk
  • Futaoki: Hishaku stand or lid rest
  • Chashaku: Tea scoop
  • Chakin: Linen cleaning cloth
  • Chawan: Tea bowl
  • Kensui: Waste bowl
  • Fukusa: Silk napkin
